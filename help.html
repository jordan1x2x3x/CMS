manage_accountsAccount & Login
How do I create an account?expand_more
Click Create an Account on the login page. You'll go through 3 steps: select your role, fill in your profile details (name, email, matric number for students), then set a password. Students do not need an access code — only officers, admins, and super admins do.
What is an access code and where do I get one?expand_more
Access codes are one-time codes required for non-student roles (Officers, Admin, Super Admin). They are issued by the ICT Unit / Super Admin. Contact the ICT office at the Senate Building to request yours. Each code can only be used once.
I can't sign in — it says "incorrect credentials".expand_more
Make sure you are using the correct email (e.g. name@mtu.edu.ng) or matric number, the right role, and your correct password. If you just registered, your account email may not be confirmed yet — contact ICT Support for help activating your account.
Can I log in with my matric number instead of email?expand_more
Yes. Students can enter their matric number (e.g. 22010301062) in the login field. The system will look up your registered email automatically. Make sure your matric number was entered correctly during signup.
I forgot my password. What do I do?expand_more
Click Forgot Password? on the login page and enter your email address. A reset link will be sent to your inbox. If you do not receive it within a few minutes, check your spam folder or contact ICT Support.
My account says "deactivated". Who do I contact?expand_more
Deactivated accounts are managed by the Super Admin / ICT Unit. Visit the ICT office at the Senate Building with your student ID to have your account reactivated.
task_altClearance Process
How does the clearance process work?expand_more
After submitting your clearance request, it is sent to all 15 departments simultaneously. Each department officer reviews your request and either Approves, Flags, or Rejects it. You are fully cleared only when all 15 departments approve you. Track your progress on the Clearance Tracking page.
What do the different statuses mean?expand_more
Pending — The department officer has not reviewed your request yet.
Approved — That department has cleared you.
Flagged — There is an issue that needs to be resolved (e.g. missing document). Check the officer's reason and resubmit.
Rejected — Your request was denied by that department. Read the reason carefully and contact the department.
My request was flagged. What do I do?expand_more
Go to the Clearance Tracking page, find the flagged department, and read the officer's reason. Upload the required document or resolve the issue, then resubmit. The officer will review again after your resubmission.
What does each department require for clearance?expand_more
Requirements vary by department. Key ones include:

HOD: 4 project copies, 4 CDs, plagiarism results, HOD & Supervisor signatures.
CEDGS: Fee payment receipt, HOD signature, Poise/Entrepreneurship proof.
Chapel: Attendance record (max 10 misses).
Bursary: School fee receipt (proof of full payment).
Library: Requires CEDGS, HOD, and College Officer clearance first.
Alumni: Proof of Alumni fee payment.
How do I get my final clearance certificate?expand_more
Once all 15 departments have approved your request, your final clearance certificate becomes available on the Certificate page of your dashboard. You can download it as a PDF. Keep this certificate safe — it is your official proof of clearance.
How long does clearance take?expand_more
The average review time is 1–2 working days per department once all required documents are submitted. Departments with outstanding dues or missing documents take longer. You can monitor real-time progress on your dashboard.
badgeFor Department Officers
How do I sign in as a department officer?expand_more
On the login page, enter your staff email and select Department Officer as your role. Use the password you set during registration. If you have not registered yet, contact the ICT Unit for an access code specific to your department.
I can only see my department's requests — is that correct?expand_more
Yes. This is by design. Each officer only sees clearance requests for their assigned department. You cannot view or modify requests from other departments.
What is the difference between Flag and Reject?expand_more
Flag — Use this when the student needs to provide more information or fix something (e.g. missing document). The student can resubmit after resolving the issue.

Reject — Use this when the student does not meet the clearance requirement and cannot be cleared at this time (e.g. outstanding debt, disciplinary issue).
Can I change my decision after approving or rejecting?expand_more
Yes. Go back to the student's review page and select a new action. Your most recent decision overrides the previous one. All changes are recorded in the audit log.
buildTechnical Issues
The page keeps redirecting me back and forth.expand_more
This is usually a session issue. Try clearing your browser cache, then log in again. If the problem persists, open the browser console (F12) and report the error to ICT Support.
My document upload is failing.expand_more
Ensure your file is in PDF, JPG, or PNG format and is under 5MB. If the upload still fails, try a different browser (Chrome or Edge recommended) or check your internet connection.
The portal says my email is not confirmed.expand_more
Your account was created but the email verification step was not completed. Contact the ICT Unit at the Senate Building — they can manually confirm your account so you can log in.
Which browsers are supported?expand_more
The portal works best on Google Chrome, Microsoft Edge, and Mozilla Firefox (latest versions). Internet Explorer is not supported. For mobile, use Chrome on Android or Safari on iOS.
